01 — Platform Mismatch
—
Shopify is the wrong tool for this business
Shopify is built for B2C e-commerce — quick add-to-cart, checkout flows, and impulse purchases. Weaver Outdoors sells heavy outdoor equipment and tractors at prices starting at $3,500. Nobody is completing a purchase like that online with a credit card.
Zero orders have been placed on their Shopify store since the site launched in January. The checkout functionality is not being used and, given the nature of the products, it won't be.
—
What they actually need is a lead capture website
High-ticket equipment sales are closed by a sales rep over the phone or in person — not through a checkout button. Every page of the site should be designed to get a potential buyer to call, fill out a form, or request more information. That's a fundamentally different goal, and it requires a different platform.
02 — The Right Benchmark
—
Riggs Direct (riggsdirect.com) is the clearest model we have
Riggs Direct is a multi-location outdoor power equipment dealer in Indiana whose site is purpose-built for this exact type of business. Their site does the following well:
  • Phone number is prominent and visible on every single page
  • Equipment organized into clean categories — tractors, mowers, track loaders, TLBs, mini excavators, UTVs
  • Every listing has a "Request More Information" form — no checkout button
  • A payment calculator lets buyers self-qualify before they even call
  • The entire experience drives phone calls and inquiry submissions
Riggs Direct runs on Dealer Spike — a SaaS platform built specifically for equipment dealers. It's well-suited for this industry but pricing is not publicly listed. Based on comparable platforms, retainers likely run $400–$700/month for the website alone, with marketing add-ons on top. It's also a closed system — limited design flexibility and no GoHighLevel integration.
03 — Our Build
—
WordPress + WooCommerce (catalog mode) + GoHighLevel
We build their website on WordPress with WooCommerce running as a catalog — no checkout, just listings — connected to GoHighLevel for all lead management. Same functionality as Dealer Spike, full design control, we own the client relationship.
  • All 24 Shopify products migrated, renamed, categorized, and tagged into a clean catalog
  • No checkout — every listing has an inquiry form feeding directly into their GHL sub-account
  • Payment calculator embedded on product pages, just like Riggs Direct
  • Phone number prominent in header, footer, and on every listing page

04 — Catalog Audit & Naming Convention
—
We need a client call before we migrate anything
The existing 24 Shopify listings are inconsistent. One product is simply listed as "1999 Simplicity Legacy 886HRS" — nothing in the title tells you it's a tractor. At scale, this makes the catalog impossible to navigate and hurts SEO. We need to agree on a standard naming format with the client before the build begins.
Recommended format:
[Year] [Brand] [Model] – [Equipment Type] – [Condition]

Example: 1999 Simplicity Legacy 886HRS – Garden Tractor – Pre-Owned
—
Why this matters
Consistent naming lets category pages — Tractors, Mowers, Track Loaders, TLBs, Mini Excavators — populate cleanly without manual cleanup. It also improves SEO, since product titles are one of the strongest signals on any catalog-style site. The client call also confirms whether the 24 listings are their full inventory or just a starting point, so we can plan the structure to scale.
05 — Scalable Opportunity
—
This build becomes a productized package for equipment dealers nationwide
If we document the process — the WordPress setup, WooCommerce catalog configuration, GHL sub-account automations, payment calculator, and inquiry forms — we have a repeatable package we can sell to other outdoor power equipment and heavy equipment dealers across the country. Dealer Spike charges these dealers $400–700/month for a templated site with no marketing integration and no CRM. We can deliver a better-looking, better-converting product at a competitive price.
This is the same model we're already executing in the dry cleaning vertical. Weaver Outdoors is the proof of concept. Once it's live and performing, we have the case study and the package ready to sell.
